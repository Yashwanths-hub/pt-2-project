PROBLEM OVERVIEW
The Hidden Risk in Every Rep
Gym injuries rarely happen without warning. Mechanical instability and 
fatigue -induced form breakdown appear before visible failure —but 
human supervision can't catch these subtle changes consistently.
Current approaches fall short:
•Human trainers provide limited, reactive supervision
•Camera systems raise privacy concerns in shared spaces
•Wearables are impractical for multi -user gym environments
The gap: No real -time hardware system exists that monitors equipment -
level data to detect approaching failure risk.

DETECTION LOGIC
Four -Stage Failure Risk Detection
Baseline Capture
System records first 3 –5 repetitions to establish baseline force profile, timing patterns, and curve characteristics
Continuous Analysis
Every subsequent rep is compared against baseline metrics to detect deviations in real -time
Fatigue Detection
Algorithm identifies force drop, increased rep duration, and small oscillations → Yellow Alert
Failure Risk
Sudden force dip and high instability trigger immediate warning → Red Alert + Buzzer
Real -World Detection Example
1 Reps 1–5: Normal State
Smooth force curve, stable timing, consistent pattern → Green LED
2 Reps 6–8: Fatigue Onset
Slight force oscillations, longer rep duration, early instability signs → 
Yellow LED
3 Reps 9–10: Failure Risk
Sudden force drop, loss of control, high variance → Red LED + Buzzer Alert
Key insight: The system provides warning before actual mechanical failure 
occurs, giving users time to stop safely and prevent injury.
